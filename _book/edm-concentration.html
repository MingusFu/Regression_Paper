A Scientific Way to Build a Likable Studying Music Playlist

2.1 EDM & Concentration

Both academic and industrial reports indicate that electronic dance music (EDM), including its subgenres like New Age/ambient EDM and trance/bass music, are frequently chosen as study music by users [e.g.,Bosk (2022); Dov (2020); Masha (2021);]. These reports also highlight that ambient EDM music is often recommended as an optimal choice for study music because: 1) it typically has few or no lyrics, making it less distracting during study sessions; and 2) it is preferred by those who do not favor other types of instrumental music, such as classical music (Masha 2021).

Additionally, evidence from experimental studies suggests that there are optimal levels of tempo and energy in songs that influence their effectiveness in improving memory and learning (e.g., Musliu et al. 2017).

Therefore, it can be assumed that: - H1: Compared to other genres, EDM generally has a higher level of instrumentalness and a lower level of speechiness.

edm<-subset(spotify, playlist_genre=="edm")
other_genre <- anti_join(spotify, edm, by = "playlist_genre")
t.test(edm$instrumentalness,other_genre$instrumentalness,paired=FALSE,alternative="greater")
